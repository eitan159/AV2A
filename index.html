Adapting to the Unknown: Training-Free Audio-Visual Event Perception with Dynamic Thresholds

1Bar-Ilan University 2Tel-Aviv University 3NVIDIA
Paper Code BibTeX

Training-free, open-vocabulary audio-visual event perception with score-level early fusion and dynamic thresholds.

Abstract

We address audio-visual event perception—temporal localization and classification across audio and visual modalities. Existing approaches are constrained by fixed vocabularies and often rely on late fusion, limiting multimodal interactions and adaptability to evolving videos. We propose a training-free, model-agnostic framework (AV2A) that (1) performs score-level early fusion and (2) adapts via a within-video label-shift mechanism with dynamic, time-varying thresholds derived from prior frames. AV2A provides a training-free, open-vocabulary baseline for audio-visual event perception and yields consistent gains when layered on state-of-the-art weakly supervised methods—without additional training.

Method at a Glance

  • Score-level early fusion (weighted sum) preserves richer multimodal interactions vs. late fusion.
  • Within-video label shift dynamically adjusts per-class thresholds over time from prior-frame evidence.
  • Training-free & open-vocabulary: plug in foundation models (e.g., CLIP/CLAP, LanguageBind) with no extra training.
